# Foshan Nanhai AI Medical System Achieves 20% Error Reduction After Serving 126,700 Patients

> Published May 31, 2026 | Source: leiphone_ai

In January 2026, Foshan Nanhai District in China unveiled an AI-native smart medical system developed through a collaborative effort between Foshan Nanhai District People's Hospital, Tianrui Medical Health, and Inspur Information. This system addresses key barriers in AI healthcare implementation, such as data security, misaligned technology needs, and collaboration challenges. After deployment, it has served over 126,700 patients, reducing error rates by 20% and improving medical record quality by 60%. The broader 'one body, two wings, three wheels drive' framework emphasizes sustainable AI integration, potentially offering a replicable model for enhancing healthcare efficiency and patient outcomes.

## Key Points

- A tripartite collaboration between Foshan Nanhai District People's Hospital, Tianrui Medical Health, and Inspur Information created an AI-native smart medical system to overcome AI healthcare implementation barriers.
- The system has demonstrated significant clinical impact by serving 126,700 patients with a 20% reduction in error rates and a 60% improvement in medical record quality.
- This model provides a replicable framework for AI adoption in healthcare, focusing on data security, clinical needs, and ecosystem collaboration to drive scalable improvements.
