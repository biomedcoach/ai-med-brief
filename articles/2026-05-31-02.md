# DeepSeek AI Limits Re-generation and Modification to 3-6 Times Amid High Demand

> Published May 31, 2026 | Source: 36kr_ai

DeepSeek, a Chinese AI company, began restricting re-generation and modification counts in late May 2026 due to high user demand and computational strain, as reported by the 'Letter AI' outlet. Users experienced limits of 3-6 re-generation attempts and 6 modifications before encountering a cap, primarily affecting expert mode with fewer chances. This temporary measure stems from server overload, where each re-generation or modification triggers a new inference request, consuming significant resources. DeepSeek has not officially confirmed the changes, but anticipates relief with new hardware deployments from Huawei Ascend chips later in 2026. The company's popularity, highlighted by a 700 billion RMB funding round, underscores the demand driving such constraints, mirroring industry trends where AI providers manage resource-intensive features to maintain basic service availability.

## Key Points

- DeepSeek implemented temporary limits on re-generation and modification to 3-6 times to manage server load and prioritize core functionality.
- High user demand and computational costs necessitate these restrictions, reflecting a common industry practice in AI services to balance accessibility with resource conservation.
- This move, while not explicitly medical, aligns with broader AI trends where companies restrict high-consumption features to ensure stability, potentially indicating future monetization strategies.
